Historical Background

Chang'e Flying to the Moon" is a beloved Chinese myth, embodying the blend of historical, cultural, and astronomical elements of ancient China. This legend is intricately woven into the fabric of Chinese folklore and is celebrated during the Mid-Autumn Festival, a significant cultural and lunar harvest festival in China.

Character Role in the Myth
Chang'e Moon Goddess, central figure of the myth
Hou Yi Skilled archer, husband of Chang'e

Key Themes

The Metaphor of Chang'e

The legend of Chang'e, the Moon Goddess in Chinese mythology, is more than just a mythical story; it's a profound metaphor that encapsulates themes of longing, consequence, and the human condition.
