Top 10 Chinese Myths

  1. Pangu Creates the World: The giant Pangu emerged from a cosmic egg and created the earth and sky by separating Yin from Yang.
  2. Nüwa Mends the Heavens: Nüwa, the compassionate goddess, created humans and repaired the sky to save them from calamities.
  3. The Eight Immortals: A group of legendary immortals, each with unique powers, who represent different life conditions and virtues.
  4. Journey to the West: The epic tale of the Monkey King, Sun Wukong, and his journey with a monk to retrieve sacred texts.
  5. The Cowherd and the Weaver Girl: A love story about two star-crossed lovers separated by the Milky Way, allowed to meet just once a year.
  6. The Dragon King's Daughter: The tale of a mortal man who marries a dragon princess and the adventures that ensue.
  7. Chang'e Flying to the Moon: The story of Chang'e who swallowed the elixir of immortality and ascended to the moon.
  8. Hou Yi Shooting the Suns: Hou Yi, the archer, who saved the world from burning by shooting down nine of the ten suns.
  9. The Legend of the White Snake: A poignant love story between a mortal man and a snake spirit transformed into a beautiful woman.
  10. Jiang Ziya Fishes for Kings: The wise Jiang Ziya who used a straight hook to fish, symbolizing the proper way to select able rulers.

Pangu separating the earth and sky

The myth of Pangu is a creation story, symbolizing the genesis of the world from chaos to order. It highlights the yin-yang duality and the continuous cycle of creation and destruction.

The romantic meeting of the Cowherd and the Weaver Girl

This touching tale symbolizes eternal love and the longing of separated lovers. It's celebrated in the Qixi Festival
